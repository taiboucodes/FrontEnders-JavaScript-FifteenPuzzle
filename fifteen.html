The goal of the fifteen puzzle is to un-jumble its fifteen squares by repeatedly making moves that slide squares into the empty space.


American puzzle author and mathematician Sam Loyd is often falsely credited with creating the puzzle;
Loyd claimed from 1891 until his death in 1911 that he invented it. The puzzle was actually created around 1874
by Noyes Palmer Chapman, a postmaster in Canastota, New York.
